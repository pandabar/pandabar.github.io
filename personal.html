Background

  • This is how I would pronunce my name in a formal context. A more relaxed pronunciation would be something like this. This one would be the phonological representation, according to the Chilean Spanish inventory.
  • I was born and raised in Concepción, Chile. I spent a semester studying abroad in St. Cloud, MN (USA) in 2006, and moved to Manchester (UK) in 2013.
  • My hometown does not look like the city that was shown in the movie The Reaping.
  • As any Chilean, I have proudly survived a big earthquake.
  • Despite my studies in Second Language Acquisition and Second Language Phonology, I have a Latin accent that tries to resemble Midwestern American English.
  • My native language is Chilean Spanish. At first sight, it has absolutely nothing to do with any variation of the Spanish spoken in Spain. If you are a phonetician with an interest in this variety, here is a link to an amazing article about the phonetic inventory of Chilean Spanish, written by Scott Sadowsky and Gastón Salamanca. My friend Mauricio Figueroa has also written very interesting articles about the subject.

Interests (other than Linguistics).

Whenever I'm not working, I spend my time either sitting or moving very fast. My quiet times involve reading literature (from Latin to contemporary classics), drawing and painting. Otherwise, I am either swimming or dancing: after having spent many years dancing flamenco, I am currently dancing salsa (Cuban style) and learning the basics of Lindy hop and Balboa.
